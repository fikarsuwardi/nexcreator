# Outreach Message Templates — Nex Entertainment

> **For candidates:** These are real outreach templates that have performed well for our recruitment team. Your tool should be able to generate personalized variations of these — not copy them verbatim, but capture the tone, structure, and key value propositions while adapting to each creator's specific profile.

---

## Template 1: TikTok DM — Casual / Enthusiastic

**Channel:** TikTok Direct Message
**Tone:** Casual, enthusiastic, creator-to-creator energy
**Best for:** Nano creators (< 10K followers), younger demographics, creators who post frequently and seem passionate about their niche

**When it works:** This template has the highest response rate (~35%) for creators under 10K followers. The short format respects the DM medium, and the specific content callout shows we actually watched their videos.

```
Hey {first_name}! 👋

Just came across your {specific_content_reference} video and honestly it's exactly the kind of content that crushes it on TikTok GO.

We're Nex Entertainment — we work with TikTok to help creators like you earn money from your {vertical_content_type} content. Our top creators are making {earnings_reference} per month just by tagging hotels/restaurants/attractions in their posts.

Would love to chat about getting you set up — it literally takes 5 mins and you keep doing what you're already doing, just with the chance to earn from it.

Interested? 🙌
```

**Personalization fields:**
- `{first_name}` — creator's display name or first name
- `{specific_content_reference}` — reference a specific recent video (e.g., "that Bali villa tour", "your Bandung street food series")
- `{vertical_content_type}` — "hotel/travel", "food/restaurant", or "attraction/tourism" based on their vertical
- `{earnings_reference}` — use tier-appropriate numbers: "$500+" for promising, "$2,000+" for rising stars, "$5,000+" for star potential

## Template 2: Email — Professional / Value-Focused

**Channel:** Email
**Tone:** Professional, data-driven, business opportunity framing
**Best for:** Micro creators (10K–50K followers), creators who already have brand partnerships, creators with email in bio

**When it works:** This template works best (~25% response rate) for creators who are already semi-professional about their content. The specific numbers and brand name-drops establish credibility. The "no upfront cost" line addresses the most common objection.

```
Subject: Partnership opportunity — earn from your {vertical} content on TikTok GO

Hi {first_name},

I'm {recruiter_name} from Nex Entertainment. We're TikTok's #1 partner agency for TikTok GO — the local services marketplace on TikTok — and we manage over 5,000 creators across Indonesia.

I've been following your content and your {specific_strength} really stands out. Creators with your posting frequency and engagement level typically see strong results on our platform.

Here's how it works:
• You keep creating the {vertical_content_type} content you already make
• We help you tag relevant businesses (hotels, restaurants, attractions) using TikTok GO
• When your viewers book or purchase through your content, you earn commission
• Our top creators in {vertical} are earning {earnings_range} per month

There's no upfront cost, no exclusivity requirement, and no minimum posting commitment. We provide the tools, the POI database, and ongoing support to help you maximize your earnings.

A few of the brands and venues our creators work with: {brand_examples}.

Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call this week to walk through the setup? I can also send over some case studies from creators in your niche.

Best,
{recruiter_name}
Nex Entertainment
{recruiter_email}
```

**Personalization fields:**
- `{first_name}` — creator's name
- `{recruiter_name}` / `{recruiter_email}` — the recruiter sending the message
- `{vertical}` — ACC, FNB, or TTD
- `{vertical_content_type}` — "hotel and travel", "food and dining", or "tourism and attractions"
- `{specific_strength}` — personalized observation (e.g., "consistency — 4 posts a week for the last 3 months", "eye for hidden gem restaurants", "production quality on your hotel tours")
- `{earnings_range}` — tier-appropriate: "$500–2,000" for promising, "$2,000–5,000" for rising stars, "$5,000–15,000" for star potential
- `{brand_examples}` — relevant brand names by vertical (ACC: "Tiket.com, Trip.com, major hotel chains"; FNB: "KFC, Domino's, Mixue, Kopi Kenangan"; TTD: "Tiket.com attractions, theme parks")

## Template 3: TikTok DM — Follow-Up (No Response)

**Channel:** TikTok Direct Message
**Tone:** Light, non-pushy, adds new value
**Best for:** Creators who didn't respond to the first message within 5–7 days
**Timing:** Send 5–7 days after first message

**When it works:** This follow-up recovers ~15% of non-responders. The key is adding a new angle (the specific earnings example or the "other creators in your area" social proof) rather than just repeating the first message.

```
Hey {first_name}! Following up on my message from last week 😊

Just wanted to share — one of our creators in {their_region} who makes similar {vertical_content_type} content just hit {recent_success_metric} last month through TikTok GO. Your content style is really similar to theirs when they first started with us.

No pressure at all — just thought you'd want to know the opportunity is there. Happy to answer any questions if you're curious!
```

**Personalization fields:**
- `{first_name}` — creator's name
- `{their_region}` — the creator's city/region
- `{vertical_content_type}` — their content type
- `{recent_success_metric}` — a real (but anonymized) achievement: "$3,000 in commissions", "200 orders from a single video", "50K views on their first tagged post"

## Guidelines for AI-Generated Variations

When your tool generates outreach messages using Claude, ensure the following:

### Must have:
1. **A specific reference to the creator's actual content** — generic "love your content!" is an instant credibility killer. Reference a specific video, series, or content pattern.
2. **Relevant earnings/opportunity framing** — calibrate the numbers to the creator's tier. Don't promise $15K/month to a creator with 1K followers.
3. **Clear value proposition** — what do they get, how does it work, what's the time commitment.
4. **Low-friction CTA** — "interested?" or "quick 10-min call" not "fill out this 20-field application form."

### Must avoid:
1. **AI-sounding language** — phrases like "I'd love to explore synergies", "leverage your platform", "exciting opportunity" are dead giveaways.
2. **Overpromising** — never guarantee specific earnings. Use ranges and say "top creators earn..."
3. **Excessive emojis** — 1–2 max for DMs, zero for emails.
4. **Wall of text in DMs** — TikTok DMs should be under 150 words. Save the details for email.
5. **Copy-paste feel** — if you removed the creator's name and it could apply to anyone, it's not personalized enough.

### Tone calibration by channel:
- **TikTok DM:** Casual, short, peer-to-peer. Like messaging a friend about a cool opportunity.
- **Email:** Professional but warm. Not corporate stiff. Think "startup founder reaching out" not "HR form letter."
- **Instagram DM:** Visual reference ("saw your reel about..."), casual, mention content crossover potential.
